5 lessons learned from making a banking chatbot (by Sidharth Garg)

reflection

Mar 1

Sidarth Garg shares his general learnings from making a banking chatbot.
One distinguishing feature of his pilot was the target market: an economically and culturally diverse set of credit union members in Brooklyn. This group of users is probably very different from the tech-savvy users of most other chatbots.
Here are his takeaways:
1. There is a lot of curiosity
Despite probably not knowing too much about chatbots beforehand, people were genuinely interested in learning more about a new “virtual banking assistant”
2. Limited exploration
Although many people understood the concept of a “banking assistant,” almost no one tried commands that weren’t suggested. Only a handful of people tried asking personal, open-ended questions.
3. Chatbots still need a human backup
He really believes that having empathy for users is crucial. That means routing the chat to a human when the conversation feels stuck.
4. Proactive reengagement
The biggest insight to come from his pilot was the need for reminders and reengagement. As with most product launches, the real challenge is user retention and growing periodic usage. This “launch curve” also applied to his pilot: Initial reception was strong but then slowly decreased. This is where smarter technology and marketing can help reverse the course.
5. Expect some strange responses
As he states:

 "
On the silly side, I got a few expected messages from people asking: “Are you human?” On the more nefarious side, I received a handful of vulgar, emoji-laden messages (some I didn’t even know existed, but I had to applaud their creativity). Perhaps I should add emoji-translation to the next version of Teller.“
